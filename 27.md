<h1>Gitlab BetterDocs</h1>

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>GitLab, on the other hand, positions itself as a complete DevOps lifecycle platform rather than just a code hosting service. While it also uses Git for version control, GitLab integrates planning, coding, testing, security, and deployment into a single application. This “all-in-one” approach reduces dependency on multiple tools, which is particularly valuable for organizations aiming for tighter control and efficiency in their development pipeline. GitLab offers both cloud-hosted and self-hosted options, giving teams more flexibility in terms of data control, security, and customization.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>From a strategic perspective, the difference is about ecosystem vs. integration depth. GitHub dominates in community, network effects, and open-source visibility—making it ideal if collaboration and exposure matter most. GitLab, however, is stronger for organizations prioritizing end-to-end DevOps control and internal efficiency. Choosing between them isn’t about features alone; it’s about aligning with your workflow strategy: do you optimize for collaboration at scale, or operational cohesion across the entire software lifecycle?</p>
